Deep Breathing (Step-by-Step)
1) Sit tall and relax your shoulders.
2) Inhale slowly through the nose for 4 seconds.
3) Hold for 2 seconds, then exhale for 6 seconds.
4) Repeat for 5 cycles, counting each breath.
5) If thoughts wander, gently return to the count.

CBT Reframing (Explanation)
Notice the thought that feels most upsetting.
Ask if the thought is a fact or an interpretation.
Look for a more balanced, realistic alternative.
Repeat the new thought slowly and kindly to yourself.
Aim for "true enough," not "perfectly positive."

Journaling for Anxiety
Set a 5-10 minute timer and write freely.
Label the fear and describe what triggered it.
List one thing you can control and one you cannot.
Write a small next step you can take today.
End with one kind sentence to yourself.

Crisis Contact Guidance
If you feel unsafe, seek immediate help.
Contact local emergency services or a trusted person.
Go to a safe place where others are present.
In the U.S., you can call or text 988 for support.
If outside the U.S., use your local crisis line.

Progressive Muscle Relaxation
Start at your feet and gently tense for 5 seconds.
Release for 10 seconds and notice the difference.
Move upward: calves, thighs, abdomen, hands, shoulders.
Keep breathing slow and steady through each muscle group.
End by relaxing your jaw and unclenching your brow.

Self-Compassion Exercise
Place a hand on your chest and take a slow breath.
Say: "This is hard, and I am not alone."
Offer yourself the same kindness you'd offer a friend.
Name one small way you can care for yourself now.
Repeat the phrase for three breaths.

Panic Attack Response
Remind yourself: "This will pass; I am safe."
Exhale longer than you inhale to calm the body.
Ground with cold water on wrists or holding ice.
Focus on a single object and describe it in detail.
Seek support if the panic feels unmanageable.

Sleep Anxiety Coping
Dim lights and reduce screen time 30 minutes before bed.
Write down worries and park them for tomorrow.
Try a 4-7-8 breathing cycle for 3 rounds.
Keep the room cool, dark, and quiet if possible.
If awake after 20 minutes, do a calm activity.

Emotional Labeling
Name the emotion out loud: "I feel anxious/sad/angry."
Rate its intensity from 0-10 without judging it.
Notice where it shows up in your body.
Allow it to be present while you keep breathing.
Check again after a few minutes and re-rate.

Box Breathing
Inhale for 4 seconds.
Hold for 4 seconds.
Exhale for 4 seconds.
Hold for 4 seconds.
Repeat for 4 cycles, counting gently.

Cognitive Distortions (Explanation)
Distortions are thinking habits that skew reality.
Examples: all-or-nothing, catastrophizing, mind-reading.
Spot the distortion and name it without self-criticism.
Ask what evidence supports and challenges the thought.
Choose a more balanced statement.

Thought Record Method
Write the situation, emotion, and automatic thought.
Rate the belief in the thought from 0-100%.
List evidence for and against the thought.
Create a balanced alternative thought.
Re-rate the emotion and notice the change.

Safe Space Visualization
Close your eyes and imagine a place you feel safe.
Notice colors, sounds, textures, and temperature.
Breathe slowly as you explore the space in detail.
Add a supportive figure or phrase if you wish.
Return to this image whenever you need steadiness.

Distress Tolerance (DBT-Style)
Use the TIPP skills: temperature, intense exercise, paced breathing.
Change body temperature with cool water or a cold pack.
Do 30-60 seconds of brisk movement to reset arousal.
Practice slow breathing and relax your shoulders.
Then choose one small, wise next step.

Emergency Help Reminder
If you are in crisis or thinking about self-harm, get help now.
Call local emergency services or go to the nearest ER.
Reach out to a trusted person and stay with others.
In the U.S., call or text 988 for immediate support.
If outside the U.S., use your local crisis resources.
